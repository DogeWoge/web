2016-05-22

After a year of not doing anything music-related, I forgot why I chose Rosegarden to replace Finale, which doesn't run on Linux. Its distinguishing characteristic seems to be its MIDI sequencer, but I'm not a MIDI sequencer. Then I discovered that, since Rosegarden uses LilyPond to print its music anyway, I might as well work with that directly.

It's the first day and it's amazing. The syntax feels so clean and pure, there's no danger of accidentally moving something that screws up the layout a hundred bars later, and I immediately took advantage of wrapping a 8-bar melody in a variable and simply referring to that variable instead of copying all eight bars over and over again! There's no way I'll ever go back to Rosegarden or even Finale, whose WYSIWYG editors often give me what I'd rather not get, and all the time wasted dragging and misclicking and reformatting might really be slower than directly typing in the notes. All you need is LilyPond, and a modern text editor, and a PDF reader, and a MIDI synthesizer, and maybe throw it into version control to boot.

2016-06-03

Sokoban was a bad idea. Unlike sudoku or sokoban, there's no obvious beginner strategy (fill all cells that have only one valid choice; mine or flag all neighbouring cells if the numbers match) or even a guaranteed strategy (brute-force search through all valid boards; flag all the mines and mine everything else) since the search space is so big. All I could do was implement the game itself.

In other words, sol can't solve it. I'm quite disappointed in myself. After an entire month exploring Lisp & friends, I didn't apply anything new except for useless GUI manipulations and superficial syntax differences. I didn't use any macros, any file I/O features, or any object-oriented aspects of (especially Common) Lisp. I still think Emacs is ridiculously awkward and too memory-intensive.

But I didn't completely waste my May. I've spent quite a while really trying to grok C pointers and I think I'm good enough to start working on FTN, a Nethack clone right down to the language I'll code it in—not C++, not C#, not D, but good ol'-fashioned C.
